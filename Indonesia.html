Flag

The national flag of Indonesia is a simple bicolor with two horizontal bands, red (top) and white (bottom). It was introduced and hoisted in public during the proclamation of independence on 17 August 1945. The design of the flag has remained unchanged since.

The flag of Indonesia is graphically similar to the flag of Monaco, with a slight difference in the shade of red, and ratio of its dimensions. The flag of Poland has similar dimensions but has the colours reversed: white on top and red on the bottom. In both Monaco's and Poland's flags, the reds are of a slightly darker shade than that of Indonesia.

Several opinions have been expressed on the meaning of the red and white in the Indonesian flag. One of them is that the red stands for courage, while the white stands for purity. Another opinion is that red represents the human body or physical life, while white represents the soul or spiritual life; together they stand for a complete human being.

Island

Indonesia is a country in Southeast Asia and Oceania between the Indian and Pacific oceans. It consists of over 17,000 islands, including Sumatra, Java, Sulawesi, and parts of Borneo and New Guinea. Furthermore, Indonesia has a total of 38 provinces. Indonesia is the world's largest archipelagic state and the 14th-largest country by area, at 1,904,569 square kilometres (735,358 square miles).

Nature

Indonesia is a tropical archipelago graced with more nature than one person could enjoy in a lifetime. A home to gorgeous beaches and islands, rich culture and eco-diversity, Indonesia scores high on the scale for popular destinations. The mesmerizing nature and fascinating culture have pushed the country high on the list of coveted places to visit in the world. Among hundreds of charms the country has, here’s some natural wonders in Indonesia that will take your breath away.

Bali Island

Bali Island is one of the most beautiful islands in the world. Bali has several tourist attractions that are already well-known throughout the world such as Kuta Beach, Sanur Beach, Kintamani, Lake Batur, Gua Gajah, Tampak Siring, Bedugul, Ubud, Tanah Lot and many more. This island is included in the Ten Natural Wonders of Indonesia because of the many amazing places with its unique architecture, culture and natural beauty.

Mount Bromo

Mount Bromo is one of the most visited tourist attractions in Indonesia. The active volcano sits in the middle of a plain called the Sea of Sand (Segara Wedi), along with several other mountains. This active volcano has an iconic blown-off top from recurring eruptions, resulting in a stunning mountain landscape emitting sulfurous smoke, one of the most popular and photographed scenes from Indonesia.

Komodo Island

Widely known as a habitat to the curious and ancient Komodo dragons, this island’s natural beauty matches its wildlife wonder. It has a picturesque landscape of hills, lush vegetations, exotic coastlines, and clear blue ocean, all in one glance. Any point in the island, be it the otherworldly Pink Beach or the hilltop overlooking the ocean and nearby small islands, is a natural wonder like nowhere else in the world.

Population

The population of Indonesia was 270.20 million according to the 2020 national census, an increase from 237.64 million in 2010. Indonesia is the fourth most populous country in the world. Approximately 55% of Indonesia's population resides on Java, which is the most populous island in the world. This rapid population growth has led to a myriad of challenges, and one of the most prominent issues is the alarming rise in traffic congestion. The surge in urbanization and economic activities in major cities has resulted in an unprecedented increase in the number of vehicles on the roads.

Borobudur Temple

Borobudur is a Buddhist temple located in Magelang, Central Java, Indonesia. This stupa-shaped temple was founded by the adherents of Buddhism around the year 800 AD during the reign of Wangsa Syailendra. Borobudur is the largest Buddhist temple in the world and has been recognized as one of the seven wonders of the world and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
